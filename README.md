## About the Task

Phishing is one of the most common and damaging cyber threats faced by modern organizations. Instead of exploiting technical vulnerabilities, phishing attacks rely on **social engineering** to trick users into clicking malicious links, downloading harmful attachments, or disclosing sensitive information such as passwords and one-time codes.

This task focuses on analyzing realistic phishing email examples and presenting the findings in a **clear, business-friendly manner**. The goal is not to hack or exploit systems, but to **educate users, explain risk, and reduce human-based security threats**.

This task simulates the role of a **Security Analyst / Security Awareness Analyst** and follows ethical and professional security standards.

---

## Objective

The objectives of this task are to:

- Analyze phishing email examples (public or simulated)
- Identify common phishing indicators and social-engineering techniques
- Classify email risks as **Safe**, **Suspicious**, or **Phishing**
- Explain phishing attacks using simple, non-technical language
- Provide clear prevention and awareness guidance for users
- Produce a professional, client-ready awareness report

---

## Scope & Ethics

This assessment was conducted strictly for **educational and awareness purposes**.

### In Scope
- Analysis of phishing email content
- Review of sender information and domains
- Inspection of embedded links (no interaction)
- Identification of phishing indicators
- Risk classification and awareness documentation

### Out of Scope
- Sending real phishing emails
- Exploitation of user accounts or systems
- Malware creation or execution
- Credential harvesting
- Any action that could cause harm or disruption

All email samples used are **simulated or publicly available examples**.

## Analysis Methodology

The phishing email analysis followed a structured approach:

1. Collection of phishing email samples (public or simulated)
2. Review of email subject lines, sender details, and message content
3. Conceptual analysis of email headers
4. Inspection of sender domains and embedded URLs
5. Identification of phishing indicators and social-engineering patterns
6. Classification of email risk levels
7. Documentation of findings in clear, business-friendly language
8. Development of prevention and awareness guidelines
